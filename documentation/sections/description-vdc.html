This section presents some examples of usage of VDC, serialized in turtle. A driving context is composed of events and states about the following features of interests: the driver and passengers, the vehicle and the spatial region.

Driving states

A driving state is a subclass of sosa:ObservableProperty, that can produce sosa:Observation instances at certain time (time:TemporalEntity) and space (geo:SpatialThing). A vehicle is an instance of a vsso:Vehicle. It has a certain number of vsso:attribute and vsso:ObservableSignal. A vsso:ObservableSignal is a state of the vehicle. A driver is a foaf:Person that was attributed the role of vdc:Driver. A passenger was attributed the role vdc:Passenger. All people have a vdc:MentalState and a vdc:BehavioralState. The vdc:MentalState class contains all emotions and mental states (stress, distraction...), while the vdc:BehavioralState class contains all behavior of drivers and passengers. These classes are mapped with the MFOEM and SIO ontologies. A vdc:SpatialRegion can be a vdc:Road or a vdc:Area. The vdc:Road is the road the vehicle is on. The vdc:Area is the general spatial area around the vehicle, with its features and points of interest. An additional state of the vdc:SpatialRegion is the vdc:WeatherState. It is mapped with the Weather Ontology and the SEAS ontology.

Driving events

A driving event is an subclass of event:Event. It has a certain number of foal:Agent instances (people, vehicle) involved, potential event:Factor, event:sub_event and event:Product at certain time (time:TemporalEntity) and space (geo:SpatialThing).
